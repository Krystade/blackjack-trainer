# Adversarial Red-Team of the Trainer as a Training System — 2026-07-23

The ADVERSARIAL leg of the standing workflow (see `docs/BACKLOG.md`). Two personas attack
the current app: **[PRO]** a professional counter asking "what still fails at a real table
for someone who aces every drill here?", and **[SCI]** a learning scientist attacking the
training regime itself. Every finding was verified against the code cited — nothing here is
argued from an imagined feature. Items already in `BACKLOG.md` or the 2026-07-21 priority
list are marked and not elaborated.

Not re-proposed (already queued/shipped): distraction training (BACKLOG D1), timed speed
ramp + benchmarks (`drills/countSpeed.ts`, shipped), true-count drill (shipped), deck
estimation drill (shipped), per-drill accuracy telemetry for the three counting drills
(`store/types.ts` trueCount/deckEstimation/timedCount, shipped), countdown eyes-free
support (BACKLOG D2), clip/correction wording work (D2).

## Ranked findings

| # | Persona | Finding | Evidence (verified) | Why it matters at a real table | Smallest fix | Size |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | PRO+SCI | **Nothing measures or pressures decision speed on strategy decisions.** `GradedEvent` (`src/engine/grade.ts:14-25`) has no time/latency field; table play (`src/ui/screens/Table.tsx`) gives unlimited thinking time on every action, insurance modal, and count check; flashcards and the deviation quiz are untimed. The ONLY speed measurement in the app is the timed count drill. | A learner can be 100% accurate at 5 s/decision and be table-useless — automaticity, not accuracy, is what transfers, and the app literally cannot detect its absence. Insurance is the sharpest case: the most count-sensitive call in the game, offered under a closing window at a real table, practiced here in an untimed modal. | Add `elapsedMs` to `GradedEvent` at every grading call site + surface per-category latency in Stats; optional "shot clock" toggle for table decisions/insurance. | S |
| 2 | PRO | **No wonging / back-counting entry-exit practice — the player MUST bet every round.** `Game.startRound` (`src/engine/game.ts:297`) always stakes every hand; `BET_CHIPS = [1,2,4,8,10,12]` (`src/ui/components/ActionBar.tsx:25`) has no sit-out; `DEFAULT_SPREAD` starts at `minTc:-99 → 1 unit` (`game.ts:42`), so the trainer structurally teaches playing all negative counts. | Wong-out/wong-in is the biggest EV lever in shoe games and a *decision skill* (when to leave, when to enter, re-entry count carrying). BJA's own curriculum makes unstaked back-counting the final pre-money step. Someone who aces this app has never once practiced the exit decision. | "Sit out" option in the bet phase, graded as a wong decision against TC (sit at TC ≤ wong-out threshold = correct; count keeps running while sitting out); back-count-only mode is the fuller version. | M |
| 3 | PRO | **Table TC checks are graded against exact shoe depth the UI gives you zero way to see.** `submitCountCheck` grades the TC answer vs `shoe.decksRemaining` (`src/engine/game.ts:510-547`), but `Table.tsx` renders **no discard tray or depth cue of any kind** — the player's only option is mentally tallying cards dealt, which no real counter does (they glance at the tray). Meanwhile a deck-tray visual already exists in `DeckEstimationView.tsx` (`deck-tray-fill`). | This actively *mistrains*: it grades a skill (depth awareness) while withholding the input real play provides, and it strands the deck-estimation drill as an island that never integrates with live play — the exact integration (glance → estimate → divide) that the practitioner research calls the real skill. | Render the existing tray-fill visual on the Table, fed by `shoe.cardsDealt`. | S |
| 4 | SCI | **No forgetting-curve mechanism anywhere; the one adaptive weight only ever grows.** Flashcard miss-weights are incremented on a miss and never decayed on a correct (`gradeFlashcardAnswer`, `src/ui/screens/Drills.tsx:245-270`; weight = `1 + 2·missCount` forever, `src/drills/flashcards.ts:116-119`). The deviation quiz has **no miss-weighting at all** — `drawQuizItem` samples uniformly over the 18 entries (`src/drills/deviationQuiz.ts:334-336`) despite per-index right/wrong tallies already existing in `stats.perIndex`. | A cell missed once in March is over-drilled forever; an index you got wrong five times is drawn no more often than one you've never missed. Basic strategy and indices are precisely the material that decays between casino trips — the app has telemetry to drive spaced review and doesn't use it. | XS: decay flash weight on correct answer (e.g. halve). S: weight quiz draws by `perIndex` wrong-rate. M: real scheduling (SM-2-lite) for both. | XS–M |
| 5 | SCI | **The count-peek crutch works even in test mode and is invisible to telemetry.** The TC peek button renders whenever `settings.countPeek` is on (`Table.tsx:310`) with no `feedbackMode` gate; `countPeek` defaults to `true` (`store/types.ts:96`); peeking sets only local state (`setPeeking`) and is never logged. | A learner can hold-to-peek before every deviation and bet decision, in *test mode*, and the session report scores it as clean play. "Actual play accuracy" in Stats is uninterpretable while an unmeasured answer key is available. Classic plateau trap: dashboard says table-ready, skill isn't. | Count peeks per session, show "N peeks" on the session report; disable the button when `feedbackMode === 'test'`. | XS |
| 6 | SCI+PRO | **Negative-count arithmetic is systematically undertrained.** `trueCountDrill.ts:10-11`: `RC_MIN = -6, RC_MAX = 20` — ~77% of TC-conversion questions are positive, yet floor-toward-−∞ on negatives (−5 RC ÷ 2 = **−3**, not −2) is the direction learners get wrong. The count drill draws from a fair shoe with no bias option (`makeCountDrill`, `src/drills/countDrill.ts`), so long negative stretches are rarely practiced either — CVBJ ships a Bias setting for exactly this reason. | Negative counts are where wong-out and the five negative-TC I18 indices (13v2, 12v4, 12v5, 12v6, 13v3) live; being slow/wrong below zero costs real money and the drill mix hides it. Stats' too-high/too-low breakdown can't reveal a skew the generator never samples. | XS: make the RC range symmetric (or add a "negative focus" toggle). S: count-drill shoe bias option (front-load high cards). | XS–S |
| 7 | PRO | **Cards are always presented as a neat, centered, upright, sequential stream.** `CountDrillView.tsx` renders each group in the same `count-flash-cards` container, same screen position, no rotation, no overlap; table hands render in tidy rows. CVBJ randomizes orientation and screen position, quoting: "the dealer does not face all the cards on the table in nice lines pointing towards you", and the practitioner research has counters saying home practice fails because "the glimpse is totally different". | The visual-recognition half of counting (find the cards, read a messy scatter at a glance, cancel pairs spatially) is untrained; the app trains only the arithmetic half. This is a known transfer-killer for software-trained counters. | Randomize position/rotation/slight overlap of card groups within the flash area (CSS transforms; zero engine change). | S |
| 8 | SCI | **The honor-system eyes-free path — the differentiator's default mode — records nothing.** `finishSelfCheck` (`CountDrillView.tsx:134-138`) and the selfcheck path in `TrueCountDrillView.tsx:122-136` deliberately skip all stats writes; only opt-in "strict mode" grades. | An hour of car practice a day produces zero telemetry, zero accuracy signal, zero trend line — and self-assessment without verification is systematically overconfident (the learner "feels" right). The app's flagship mode is the one mode it cannot measure or improve. | After speaking the answer, ask for a two-zone self-report tap ("got it" / "missed it") recorded as self-reported accuracy — keeps the no-keypad flow, restores a trend line. | S |
| 9 | SCI | **No progression, no interleaving, no mastery structure — five isolated grind loops.** `Drills.tsx` picker: each drill is picked manually and repeats one item type forever; difficulty knobs (group size, interval, length, max decks) are static user settings; nothing sequences basic-strategy → count → TC → deviations (the canonical progression in the landscape research), nothing gates or recommends, and practice is 100% blocked (never mixed) unless the user self-coaches. | Blocked practice inflates within-session performance and undermines retention/discrimination — the learner never practices *deciding which skill applies*, which is what a live round demands. Also lets users camp forever on the drill they're already good at. | S: "Mixed session" mode interleaving flashcard/quiz/TC items in one stream. M: a Home "today's plan" card that picks the mix from Stats weak spots. | S–M |
| 10 | SCI | **Eyes-free counting trains a different retrieval pathway than table counting — serial verbal arithmetic vs parallel visual cancellation.** `narrateCards` speaks ranks one-by-one in sequence; at `groupSize 2` the audio still forces serial add/subtract, whereas the pair-cancellation habit (3+J = skip) that the qfit/practitioner sources call essential is inherently visual/gestalt. Audio is a *perfect* medium for TC conversion (pure arithmetic) but an imperfect proxy for card counting itself. | Heavy audio-first count practice can entrench subvocalization — the slow pathway that conversation destroys (adjacent to, but distinct from, queued D1: D1 trains interference resistance; this is about which pathway gets automated in the first place). | XS: at group ≥ 2, narrate net tags with cancellation phrasing ("queen, six — cancel"; "five, four — plus two") as a card-detail option, training the chunking habit even in audio. | XS |
| 11 | PRO | **Bet grading trains the #1 surveillance tell and the app has zero cover concept.** Bet events grade `bet === expectedUnits` exactly, every round (`game.ts:335-352`) — perfect mechanical ramp conformity is the *only* rewarded behavior, and mechanical spreads are what the cover research says gets counters made. No researched tool scores camouflage either (this would be novel), but this app currently *anti-trains* it. | Someone who maxes bet accuracy here has rehearsed robotically re-sizing to the count hundreds of times — the exact pattern surveillance software flags. | XS: cover explainer/checklist (research says this is judgment content, not drillable). S: optional "cover mode" grading bets within ±1 ramp step and flagging tell patterns (e.g. raising off the top, exact-step laddering). | XS–S |
| 12 | SCI | **Count-drill grading only checks the final RC, so offsetting errors grade as perfect.** `handleRcSubmit` compares one number at the end (`CountDrillView.tsx:523-527`); a +1 slip and a −1 slip in the same run score "correct", and a wrong run tells you nothing about *where* you lost it. CVBJ logs every error with type breakdown. | Accuracy stats overestimate true per-card accuracy, and feedback is unactionable ("off by 3" — where? which card shape?). The wizardofvegas "missed cards you don't know you missed" failure mode is exactly this. | Optional mid-run checkpoints: pause at 1–2 random points, ask current RC, resume — localizes the error to a segment and catches cancellation. | S |
| 13 | SCI | **All errors are weighted equally — no EV-cost signal to prioritize practice.** `Stats` tallies right/wrong per category and per index (`store/types.ts:136-183`); a missed 16v10 (frequent, cheap) and a missed insurance at TC +6 (rare, expensive) count identically; the session report lists mistakes unranked (`useGame.ts buildReport`). CVBJ's error-cost tracking is the [VALUABLE] research gap #8. | Practice time is a budget; without cost weighting learners grind whatever they miss most often rather than what costs the most money. | Static EV-cost table (per index / per basic-strategy cell class) multiplied into the mistake report to sort "what's actually costing you". | M |

## Top 3 I'd build next, and why

1. **Decision latency telemetry + optional shot clock (#1).** It is the cheapest change
   that upgrades *every* existing drill and the table at once, it measures the one variable
   (automaticity) that the entire research corpus says predicts table survival, and it is
   the prerequisite for any future mastery gate or training plan (#9) to have something to
   gate on. Without it, findings #5 and #8 stay invisible too.
2. **Discard tray on the Table (#3).** Smallest fix that repairs an actively unfair graded
   check *and* turns the deck-estimation drill from an island into an integrated skill —
   glance, estimate, divide is the real-table loop, and the tray visual already exists in
   the codebase. Pure UI, no engine change, high realism return.
3. **Sit-out / wong practice (#2).** The largest single real-table competence hole: the app
   currently *cannot represent* the most profitable decision a shoe-game counter makes.
   M-sized, but it's an engine-honest feature (count keeps running, entry/exit graded
   against thresholds) that no drill-shaped workaround can substitute for.

Near-free honorable mention: flash-weight decay + quiz miss-weighting (#4) — a few lines
each, and they convert existing telemetry into the app's first genuinely adaptive practice.
